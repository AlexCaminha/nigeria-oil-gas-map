@Map of Nigeria Oil Field.pdf shows a very detailed map of Nigeria Oil Fields

For the explanation below all hex data means colors

Every "oil block" identification starts with OPL meaning prospection, OML meaning mining and ends with a number. 

Beneath assigned oil block is the licensee name, if any.

Every block is outlined in #000000.

Other names within the oil block outline boundary represent "oil fields". 

Oil field names are in upper case meaning a developed field and mixed case for undeveloped.

Oil fields are also outlined in black. Background colors are 
#00FF00 for Oil or Oil/Gas and 
#FF0000 for Gas or Gas/Condensate.

Oil block background color has "status":
* #FFFF00 means "Returned to NNPC".
* #BEFFB0 means "Onshore licensed".
* #DBFFD1 means "Onshore relinquished".
* #FFFFBE means "Onshore open".
* #A1FFFF means "Offshore licensed".
* #FFFFBE means "Offshore relinquished"
* #EBFFFF means "Offshore open".

Pay attention that oil block background color shall not be confused with oil field background color. Always resolve oil field first then oil block later.

All information above is located in the page relative to the following boundaries
* Longitude is between 2 degrees East (left) and 10 degrees East (right)
* Latitude is between 2 degrees North (bottom) and 8 degrees North (top)

Create a .CSV file with these columns
* "oil block number"
* oil block "licensee"
* oil block "status"
* oil field name
* status "developed" or "undeveloped"
* type "Oil or Oil/Gas" or "Gas or Gas/Condensate"
* estimated Longitude
* estimated Latitude
